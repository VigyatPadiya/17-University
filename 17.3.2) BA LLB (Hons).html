BA LLB (Hons) Subjects

Constitutional Law

Constitutional Law covers the study of a country's constitution, its principles, interpretation, and the relationship between the government and citizens. It includes topics like fundamental rights, separation of powers, and constitutional amendments.

Criminal Law

Criminal Law focuses on understanding and analyzing different types of crimes, their classifications, elements, and punishments. It also explores the criminal justice system, procedures, and legal defenses.

Contract Law

Contract Law deals with the formation, interpretation, and enforcement of contracts. Students learn about essential contract elements, terms, breach of contract, remedies, and the role of contracts in various transactions.

Tort Law

Tort Law covers civil wrongs, liabilities, and remedies. It includes topics like negligence, nuisance, defamation, and strict liability. Students study how individuals can seek compensation for harm caused by others.

Family Law

Family Law examines legal issues related to marriage, divorce, adoption, child custody, and inheritance. Students gain insights into family relationships, domestic disputes, and legal processes concerning family matters.

International Law

International Law focuses on legal norms that govern relations between countries and international organizations. Students explore treaties, diplomatic immunity, state sovereignty, human rights, and international conflicts.
