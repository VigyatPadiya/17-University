LLM Program

The Master of Laws (LLM) program offers a diverse range of subjects that allow students to deepen their understanding of legal concepts and specialize in various areas of law. Below are some of the key subjects offered in the LLM program:

Constitutional Law

Constitutional Law focuses on the study of a country's fundamental legal principles, including the structure of government, individual rights, and the interpretation of constitutional documents.

International Law

International Law examines the legal principles that govern relations between states and international organizations. It covers topics such as treaties, human rights, diplomacy, and international dispute resolution.

Corporate Law

Corporate Law delves into the legal aspects of business organizations, including formation, management, governance, mergers and acquisitions, and corporate compliance.

Criminal Law

Criminal Law explores the principles and procedures of criminal justice, including the definition of crimes, investigation, prosecution, and the rights of defendants.

Environmental Law

Environmental Law focuses on legal issues related to environmental protection, conservation, and sustainable development. It covers areas such as pollution control, natural resource management, and climate change law.

Human Rights Law

Human Rights Law examines the protection and promotion of fundamental human rights at the national and international levels. It addresses issues of equality, discrimination, and social justice.
