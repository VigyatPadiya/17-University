BCA (Hons) Cyber Security

BCA (Hons) Cyber Security is a specialized program that focuses on the field of cybersecurity. It equips students with the knowledge and skills required to protect digital systems, networks, and data from cyber threats and attacks.

Program Subjects

  • Introduction to Cyber Security

    An overview of cybersecurity concepts, principles, and best practices. Students learn about the importance of cybersecurity in today's digital landscape.

  • Network Security

    Focuses on securing computer networks from unauthorized access, attacks, and vulnerabilities. Topics include firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and encryption.

  • Information Security Management

    Exploration of strategies for managing information security within organizations. Topics include risk assessment, policy development, and incident response.

  • Cryptography

    Study of cryptographic techniques used to secure communication and data. Students learn about encryption algorithms, digital signatures, and cryptographic protocols.

  • Ethical Hacking

    Introduction to ethical hacking and penetration testing. Students learn how to identify vulnerabilities in systems and applications to improve security.

  • Cyber Crime and Digital Forensics

    Investigation of cybercrime incidents and digital evidence collection. Students learn about forensic techniques to analyze and recover data.
